Boltz-2 Inference Speedup

How far can we push GPU inference speed without retraining — by systematically exploring step reduction, precision tuning, kernel engineering, and framework optimization?

50 orbits explored · eval-v5 (L40S GPU, 3 seeds, PDB ground truth validated) · Campaign Issue · Repository

Where the savings come from: ODE sampling provides the largest single gain (1.40×) by replacing 200 stochastic diffusion steps with 12 deterministic ODE steps. Bypassing Lightning's Trainer.predict() with direct model calls eliminates ~5s of framework overhead (strategy setup, per-batch hooks, callback dispatch). TF32 and bf16 precision are required by the bypass wrapper to produce correct structures — without them, the 12-step ODE trajectory diverges.

Speedup confirmed

Predict-only speedup is 1.61× (40.5s → 25.1s mean) across all 35 targets, consistent with the 1.69× measured on the 3-complex development set. The speedup is uniform across target sizes — it does not depend on chain count or residue count.

What Didn't Work

Every approach below was re-validated on eval-v5 stacked on the winning config (ODE-12 + TF32 + bf16 + bypass + recycle=3) with CA RMSD structural comparison. Numbers are from eval-v5 unless noted.

"Make GPU faster" — all failed

The GPU is already well-utilized by cuequivariance CUDA kernels. Every attempt to speed up existing computations backfired.

torch.compile (4 orbits)
1.00× — no speedup on predict_only
Tested on pairformer (#4), score model (#20), diffusion loop (#48), fullgraph mode (#16). cuequivariance kernels + bf16 autocast leave no room. 70–110s compilation overhead.
SDPA / Flash Attention
0.98× — eval-v5, CA RMSD identical
Pair bias tensor blocks FlashAttention-2 fused kernel; SDPA falls back to math backend. Tested across 3 orbits (#5, #19, #36).
BMM Triangle Mult
0.69× — 1.45× slower than cuequivariance
Replacing cuequivariance CUDA kernels with torch.bmm rewrite loses fused LayerNorm+Linear+sigmoid+einsum optimization. Quality identical. (#35)
INT8 Quantization
1.00× — quality-neutral but no real speedup
Simulated INT8 (quantize→dequantize) preserves quality (+0.04pp pLDDT, CA RMSD identical). But torchao's AffineQuantizedTensor is architecturally incompatible with Boltz-2's weight access patterns — real INT8 tensor cores cannot be activated. (#33, #23)
ODE Step Reduction (<12)
1.00× — predict-only flat from 8–15 steps
Diffusion is only 22% of predict-only time after bypass. Reducing from 12 to 8 steps saves ~0.5s on a 17s budget. Sharp quality cliff at 6 steps (pLDDT 0.40). (#49)
CUDA Graphs
~10 min compile
48 Pairformer layers × dynamic shapes = recompilation per input. Covered by torch.compile reduce-overhead mode in #48.

"Reduce model computation" — limited by quality

All re-validated on eval-v5 with CA RMSD. Quality regressions are consistent with or worse than earlier eval versions.

CA RMSD 2.34 Å (baseline 0.47 Å)
pLDDT passes (−0.7pp) but hemoglobin tetramer chains are globally mispositioned. Only 49.5% of residues within 2Å of ground truth. (#46, #51)

Catastrophic quality collapse. 0% of residues within 2Å on antibody-lysozyme complex. Pairformer is quality-critical. (#25)
Token Merging (10%)
−40.1pp pLDDT, CA RMSD 26.5 Å on 1DQJ
Even 10% token merge destroys pair representation. Structures are essentially random on multi-chain complexes. (#27)
Sparse Triangular Ops
Dead end (~4% of compute)
Full k-summation carries essential long-range structural info. Even if sparsification worked, triangle mult is only 4% of predict-only time — ceiling too low. (#37)
Head Pruning (75%)
1.00× — no speed gain, CA RMSD unchanged
Zeroing 75% of sequence attention heads has no effect on speed (triangle ops dominate) and no meaningful effect on structural accuracy (CA RMSD 16.4Å vs 16.5Å control, within noise). pLDDT shifts +2.2pp but this is not corroborated by RMSD. (#34)
The strategic pivot: After exhausting GPU-level and architecture-level optimizations across 50 orbits, profiling revealed that ~40% of predict-only time was framework overhead (Lightning Trainer, CUDA lazy init, batch hooks). This led directly to the bypass-lightning approach — the only optimization that delivered substantial speedup beyond ODE sampling.

Architectural Findings

The Pairformer is sacred

The 64-block Pairformer is simultaneously the quality-critical compute path (triangle operations carry all structural signal) and the optimization barrier (blocks torch.compile, TensorRT, CUDA graphs via @torch.compiler.disable). You cannot prune it, merge its tokens, sparsify its contractions, or quantize its weights without quality collapse.

cuequivariance kernels are optimal

The cuequivariance CUDA kernels for TriangleMultiplication and TriangleAttention are already faster than any alternative we tested: custom Triton kernels, torch.bmm, SDPA, inductor-generated code. The ~8% fusion advantage from fusing LayerNorm + Linear + sigmoid + einsum + gate into one kernel cannot be matched by unfused alternatives.

Recycling is structurally essential for multi-chain complexes

pLDDT is blind to multi-chain positioning errors. With 0 recycling, per-residue backbone geometry is correct (high pLDDT) but chains are globally mispositioned (high CA RMSD). At least 2 recycling passes are needed for the hemoglobin tetramer (2DN2) to achieve correct inter-chain arrangement. This means recycling reduction cannot be used as a speed optimization unless structural validation against ground truth confirms it for the specific target class.

DiffusionTransformer layers are free

Cutting from 24 to 8 DiffusionTransformer layers has zero quality or timing impact at 12 ODE steps — confirmed on eval-v5 with CA RMSD identical to 4 decimal places (#25). The score model is massively over-parameterized for inference at reduced step counts.

75% of sequence attention heads are redundant

Zeroing 75% of Pairformer sequence attention heads has no meaningful effect on structural accuracy (CA RMSD within noise) and no speed benefit (#34). pLDDT shifts +2.2pp but this is not corroborated by CA RMSD improvement — consistent with our finding that pLDDT is an unreliable proxy for structural quality. The useful signal is carried by the pairwise track (triangle attention + multiplication), not sequence attention.

MSA module can be cached across recycling (unverified)

Preliminary results suggest that caching the MSA module output from the first trunk pass and reusing it on recycling passes 2+ could reduce predict-only time by ~35%, since MSA inputs don't change across recycling iterations (#32). This signal was unexpectedly large and needs independent verification before adoption.

Method

Evaluation harness (eval-v5)

Subprocess per prediction on Modal L40S GPU. Pre-cached MSAs eliminate network latency. Phase timestamps exclude model loading from timing. Development set: three complexes with PDB experimental ground truth: Barnase-Barstar (1BRS, 2.0Å), Fab HyHEL-63 + HEWL (1DQJ, 1.80Å), Hemoglobin tetramer (2DN2, 1.25Å). Validation set: 35 CASP15 protein targets (T* + H*) spanning 44–1,439 residues, with PDB ground truth for CA RMSD. Baseline: 200 EDM/Karras steps, 3 recycling passes, fp32.

Quality gates

pLDDT gate: mean regression ≤ 2pp, per-complex ≤ 5pp. Structural gate: CA RMSD vs PDB ground truth must not regress more than 1Å beyond baseline's own accuracy. Both gates validated with 3 seeds.

Optimization approach

50 independent orbit experiments, each testing a specific hypothesis on an isolated git branch. No retraining, no weight modification, no architectural changes. All optimizations are monkey-patches applied at inference time. Final validation suite (Configs A–E) all run with recycling_steps=3 and CA RMSD structural comparison.
